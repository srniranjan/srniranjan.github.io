ML Training

Many software engineers struggle to transition into data science and ML, despite their strong programming backgrounds. The barrier isn't just math—it's the way it's presented. Most of the core mathematical concepts behind ML, like linear algebra and calculus, were already covered in high school, but they were never taught with real-world applications in mind.

My training program bridges this gap by helping engineers revisit and apply the math they already know, rather than overwhelming them with theory. More importantly, I emphasize first-principles thinking, giving them the confidence to break down complex ML concepts, go deep when needed, and learn new mathematical tools as the problem demands. By focusing on intuitive understanding and hands-on problem-solving, the program equips engineers with the foundational skills needed to navigate an AI-first world—not just as users of ML tools, but as creators who truly understand how they work.

Structured in three parts, this training systematically builds toward a deep, intuitive grasp of transformer architecture—an essential skill in today's AI-first world. Sign up to stay updated on the launch, and here's a sneak peek of what to expect!

  • Module 1: Developing the Right Mindset for Modeling ML Problems

    This module focuses on developing the foundational mindset needed to approach ML problems effectively. Unlike traditional coding, ML requires understanding how to represent problems mathematically. If we don't grasp what vectors model our problem, which space they exist in, what solution space we're aiming for, and how to transition between them, we're merely feeding numbers into an API and hoping for the best—akin to the infinite mokey theorem.

    Here, we revisit the linear algebra we've already learned and build upon it, mastering concepts like vector spaces, matrix decomposition, projections, and norms. More importantly, we apply these "lego blocks" to real ML problems, learning not just the theory but how to wield these tools effectively in practical scenarios.

  • Module 2: Deconstructing Neural Networks to Build Intuition

    Neural networks aren't magic—they're just math! This course demystifies how they work, building on your linear algebra foundation to give you a deep, hands-on understanding. You'll break down core concepts like linear regression, activation functions, and statistical learning while mastering the calculus behind backpropagation—the key to how neural networks actually learn. By the end, you won't just be following tutorials or tweaking hyperparameters blindly; you'll have the confidence to build, optimize, and truly understand neural networks, making you a far more effective AI/ML practitioner.

  • Module 3: Attention is All You Need

    LLMs are everywhere right now, but don't stress—they need not replace you. Instead, this course empowers you to take control and use them to your advantage. Building on the concepts from the previous courses, we'll dive deep into the transformer architecture and break down the groundbreaking "Attention is All You Need" paper to understand how LLMs tick. By the end, you'll not only build your own custom GPT but also have an under-the-hood understanding of LLMs, the skills to deploy them locally and distill them for your own use cases. This is the ultimate re-skilling every software engineer needs to thrive in today's LLM-powered world.
