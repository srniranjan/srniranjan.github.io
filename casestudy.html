Case Study: Building Haggle - A Personalized Pricing Engine for Mobile Commerce

Introduction

Haggle was an ambitious attempt to revolutionize mobile commerce by allowing users to negotiate personalized prices with restaurants based on their social and purchase history. As the CTO and co-founder, I led the development of a data-driven pricing engine that leveraged machine learning (ML) and social data analytics. Over a span of three years, we raised $500K in angel funding, onboarded 100 restaurants, and built a platform that saw peak Monthly Active Users (MAU) of 50,000 and Daily Active Users (DAU) of 5,000.

Problem Statement

Traditional restaurant pricing lacked personalization. Loyal customers, high-spending users, and social influencers were often treated the same as first-time visitors. Haggle sought to empower users by allowing them to prove their value to restaurants and negotiate discounts based on four key scores:

  1. History Score - Frequency of past visits and order values.
  2. Loyalty Score - Consistency in returning to the same restaurant.
  3. Social Influence Score - Online engagement, reviews, and reach.
  4. Purchasing Power Score - Spending capacity based on transaction history.

Data Collection & Scoring Model

The core challenge was developing a robust scoring model that could assess users fairly and dynamically. We collected and processed data from multiple sources:

  • Transaction Data: Users linked their payment methods, allowing us to analyze spending habits.
  • Social Media Feeds: We integrated with platforms like Instagram, Twitter, and Facebook to measure engagement and influence.
  • Restaurant Check-ins & Reviews: Data from Google Reviews, Yelp, and Foursquare helped us gauge user activity.
  • CRM & POS Integration: Partnered restaurants provided anonymized transaction logs.

Growth and user adoption

Haggle grew steadily after our initial launch, driven by organic word-of-mouth and influencer partnerships. Key metrics at peak performance:

  • 50,000 MAU, 5,000 DAU
  • 60% user retention after first transaction
  • Average discount negotiated: 8-12% per transaction
  • Conversion rate (users who completed a haggle process): 28%

Marketing efforts included partnerships with food bloggers, who demonstrated how they secured better prices using their Social Influence Score.

Challenges

  1. Cold Start Problem: New users lacked data, making it hard to assign initial scores. Solution: We implemented proxy scores based on demographic data and onboarding surveys.
  2. Data Privacy Concerns: Users were hesitant to link their social media and payments. Solution: We introduced opt-in anonymized scoring models.
  3. Restaurant Resistance: Many were skeptical of dynamic pricing. Solution: We provided analytics dashboards showing increased engagement and repeat visits.
  4. Regulatory Hurdles: Compliance with financial and data privacy laws was challenging, especially regarding payment integrations.

Conclusion

Haggle was an ambitious endeavor that demonstrated the potential of personalized pricing in the restaurant industry. While we faced challenges in scaling adoption and regulatory constraints, our ML-driven scoring engine successfully allowed users to leverage their digital footprint for better pricing. The experience of building Haggle reinforced key lessons in user behavior, data modeling, and market dynamics, which continue to inform my approach to AI-driven commerce solutions.
